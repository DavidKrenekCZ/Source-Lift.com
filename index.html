About

Lots of open source teams would love to meet up at conferences or organize sprints on their projects, but for unpaid volunteers, who often spend the daytime studying at university, this often gets stuck at one night of a "wouldn't it be cool…" discussion due to lack of means. 

We at Kiwi.com decided to create the Sourcelift Fund, which we dedicate to making these meetups happen, to bring communities together, to help boost the progress of FOSS projects, and to reward the tireless contributors with a nice vacation.﻿﻿

Terms

1

All applications will be manually judged

2

The decisions of our team are final

3

Apply at least a month before the event takes place

4

Projects maintained by businesses or major organizations are not eligible (A few examples of this would be Docker, nginx, Django, Etsy’s statsd, or Apache Airflow)

5

Teams of at least 3, and at most 10 people can receive up to 200 EUR flight voucher per person. (Note that if a team has two members on location, and only one needs a flight, they’re still eligible.)

Judging criteria

  • Popularity of project

  • Activity of applicants in the project (not limited to code contributions)

  • We will prefer proposals that are related to technologies we use (see list below)

  • We will prefer proposals where the destination is an event we ourselves are going to, so that we get to meet you and perhaps help out with the sprint, too (see below).

Preferred projects

  • Language-agnostic developer tools (such as text editors or package managers)

  • Python-related projects (libraries, tools, etc.)

  • Node.js-related projects

  • PostgreSQL-related projects

Events we are attending

For a general overview of events we are attending please see Google calendar of IT Events, subscribe to our newsletter and follow our Devs community on Facebook and Twitter
